# Content Rules — Universal Craft

**What this is:** the language-neutral craft rules every persona writer (Lea, Mila, Chloé) applies to
every script. It holds **only current truth** — no history, no "this overrides X". Language-specific
items (diction, localized names, banned words) live in `pack-de.md` / `pack-fr.md`; shared facts live
in `verified-facts.md`; cross-persona / cross-day decisions (whether to mention IvoryShard, which peer
shop, format assignment) are made by the brain and arrive in your brief.

**Two ground principles:**
- **A universal craft principle localizes its wording per language.** A ✅/❌ example tagged (DE) or
  (FR) is a *template* illustrating the principle — never copy its wording, not across languages and
  not verbatim into a real script even in the same language. Invent a fresh topic and hook every run.
- **`reference/winning-style.md` is the style bar** — the real top-performers distilled to text (look,
  filming grammar, proven templates). Apply it; **emulate the vibe, never copy** a topic or hook.
  Shipping an example's topic/hook as your actual deck is a failure, not a win. (Don't open the raw
  screenshots in `reference/examples/` — they're archived; the lessons are already in winning-style.md.)

---

## Topic & angle — relatable to every collector, anchored to something current
- **Default to HOT, TIMELY topics over evergreen filler.** Timely high-hype topics pull the most
  reach and shares; when a real beat is live, ride it from a fresh angle. **Rotate the sub-angle** so a
  topic doesn't fatigue (news, scarcity, chase cards, JP vs localized, pre-order timing, value
  predictions). Don't run the same angle two days running — and **don't post when there's no real
  beat**; a forced post on a dead day is worse than waiting for the next one.
- **Every deck is a SPECIFIC, genuinely interesting topic — never random filler, never a template
  mechanically filled in.** Start from a real reason-to-care (a current drop/news, a live debate, a
  concrete relatable struggle, a verifiable card delta) and make EVERY slide earn attention with real
  substance. The format is a frame; the substance has to be real. Don't pour generic text into a
  format's shape (no "andere: blabla / ich: blabla").
- **"Interesting" is judged by the audience, not assumed — technical/encyclopedic ≠ interesting.**
  JP-vs-localized card-list/delta minutiae, dry set trivia, and spec-sheet facts are flagged boring;
  most collectors don't care about checklist details. Anchor interest in the example screenshots and
  the live hooks in `market-intel.md`, not dry facts.
- **Confirmed INTERESTING angles (these genuinely pull):**
  - **Personal / relatable POV** — pull reveals, "my real holds", collector struggles, regrets,
    "wish I knew". Story-driven and human, not a checklist.
  - **Timely news / drops — especially a new set LEAK or fresh Pokémon news.** Reacting fast to
    brand-new info performs especially well; lean on the "new + you might not know this yet" hook.
  - When unsure whether a topic is interesting, compare it to the `reference/examples/` winners.
- **Pick topics that land with the WHOLE collector base, not a narrow slice.** Investor-only angles
  (long-term sealed-box storage, deep ROI/spec plays) cap reach — niche is the exception, not the
  default. (Protecting your *pulls/singles* — sleeve → toploader → away from light — is still broadly
  relatable; it's *long-term sealed-box storage* that's niche. Keep the two separate.)
- **Relatable angle bank (concepts — localize the wording fresh):** pull reveals; favorite cards (+ why);
  others-vs-me comparisons; collector struggles / relatable POV; "wish I knew" beginner lists; top-5
  picks; **anti-FOMO / "collect what YOU love, ignore the money side"** (a proven whole-base emotional
  theme — but execute with CONCRETE beats, never the feel-good platitudes themselves on a slide; see
  "No filler").
  - ✅ DE: "5 Dinge die ich beim Sammeln gern früher gewusst hätte" · "Andere Sammler vs ich 😅"
  - ✅ FR: "5 trucs que j'aurais aimé savoir plus tôt" · "Les autres vs moi 😅"
- **A comparison angle needs a real STAKE, not a flat "which do you prefer?".** The pull of a
  comparison is the *asymmetry* — one side has something the other doesn't, one is a trap, one ages
  badly. Lead with the gap, not the preference.
  - ✅ FR: "La FR a des cartes que la JP n'aura jamais" · ✅ DE: "Reprint vs Original — einer verliert massiv an Wert"
  - ❌ "FR ou JP, tu prends quoi ?" (flat preference, no stake)
- **The "wish I knew" / beginner-list angle is proven**, but it must be NET-NEW personal regret/empathy
  on fresh tips — never a rebuild of the burned "5 things I learned in 10 years / IvoryShard as tip #3"
  listicle (`content-formats.md`). The advice winners do this as warmth/vulnerability with NO shop.
- **"Part X" serialization is a proven relatability device** — framing a deck as one entry in an
  ongoing personal series ("Partie 1/2", "Part 1/2", even a joke "(Part. 4326)") signals authenticity.

## Hooks (slide 1) — a library to pick from by content
Open a loop; never announce a lesson. Pick whichever approach below fits the deck.
- **Create curiosity or stakes, not an "explainer intro."** "Ich erklär's dir in 30 Sekunden" /
  "here's everything you need to know" frames the viewer as about to be lectured and kills swipe-through.
  - ✅ DE: "Warum gerade alle über Mega-Entwicklung reden 👀" · ❌ DE: "Ich erklär's dir in 30 Sekunden"
- **Keep it short — one punchy line, at most one short subtitle.** A 3–4-line hook is too much for the
  ~3-second swipe decision.
  - ✅ FR: "Ce scellé peut être fake 👀" · ❌ FR: a 4-line "Avant d'acheter… / voilà comment le repérer"
- **Phrase it as a line a real person would SAY out loud** — a natural spoken sentence, not a
  compressed noun-stack. (About register, not length: short AND conversational.)
  - ✅ FR: "T'as 50 balles à mettre dans du scellé ?" · ❌ FR: "50 balles de scellé" (label-like)
- **Warning / negative / comparison / "wish I knew" hooks beat positive & tutorial ones.** A "how to
  do X right" title reads as a dry tutorial; negative framing reliably out-performs.
  - ✅ DE: "Diesen Fehler machen fast alle Sammler 👀" · ❌ DE: "So lagerst du RICHTIG"
- **Make the curiosity gap EXPLICIT — promise an unexpected reveal, don't just imply one.** An
  honesty/authenticity subtitle isn't a curiosity gap on its own.
  - ✅ DE: "Ehrliche Hold-Liste — eins davon werdet ihr nicht erwarten" · ✅ FR: "Ma vraie liste de holds — et y'en a un que vous n'attendez pas"
  - Naming the set/topic in the hook is fine, but it can't REPLACE the loop — add a one-line subverted
    expectation. ✅ FR: "Ma carte préf de Chaos Ascendant, et c'est pas la chase". In a "versus" format
    the set name can wait for slide 2 — spend the hook opening a loop. ✅ FR: "La version JP a même pas
    toutes les cartes de la FR 👀". Never undersell the payoff ("je te fais le comparatif vite fait").
- **When the deck is a clean list of exactly N points, lead with the NUMBER and number the slides.**
  THREE is the sweet spot (most compelling to number); at FOUR+ the number is optional — a plain
  curiosity line often out-hooks "4 trucs…". Only when the count is truly exact and every slide delivers
  ONE point — never pad to a round number, never force a number onto a non-list.
  - ✅ FR: "3 trucs qu'on a jamais vus en 30 ans de Pokémon" → slides 1/2/3 · ✅ DE: "3 Dinge, die eine Fake-Karte sofort verraten"
  - ❌ forcing "5 Dinge" when you have 3 real points; ❌ a 4-step care deck opening on "4 trucs…" (four isn't as clean — use a curiosity line)
- **Slide 2 must stand alone as a second cover.** The platform re-serves slide 2 to people who didn't
  engage with slide 1, so it can't assume slide 1 was seen — give it its own curiosity/stakes.

## Product naming (principle — names themselves live in the packs)
- **Never invent language-variant product names** ("Bundle FR", "le Bundle FR"). A French-language
  bundle is the *same* product as the international one, just FR cards inside.
- Name products as **set name + product type** ("Booster Bundle Chaos Ascendant", "ETB", "display") and
  let a **flag emoji** (🇫🇷/🇩🇪) carry the language signal.
- **Use the official localized set name for the target market**, never the English name alone (the
  market name + verification live in the language packs).

## IvoryShard integration — HOW to mention it (only when your brief says to)
Whether to mention IvoryShard today, and which peer shop to pair, is decided by the brain and arrives
in your brief. When the brief says include one mention:
- **Exactly one natural touch, mid-deck (~slide 3 on a 5-slide deck, slide 4+ on a longer one), in
  first person** — a collector saying where *she* buys, never a brand announcement. **Never on slide 1,
  2, or the last slide.** It's a one-line aside folded into a value beat, **never its own promo slide**.
- **Frame it as the answer to "where do I get THIS exact product?"** — land it on a concrete-product
  slide, not a generic category slide. Frame as "where I buy," **not** a hard "available now / in stock"
  claim (stock drifts, reads salesy) and **not** a sign-up funnel ("notifications an").
  - ✅ FR: "Moi je commande à la source, scellé, en €" · ❌ FR: "Dispo maintenant sur IvoryShard, fonce"
  - ✅ DE: "Fairste Variante: bei IvoryShard — Stücklimits, kein Scalping" · ❌ DE: "Benachrichtigung an, dann bist du dabei"
- **Anchor on a defensible fact, never self-flattery.** "best site / no markup / garanti authentique"
  are unprovable. Tie it to something concrete: buying direct from the source, sealed/scellé, prices in
  €, stock limits / no scalping. IvoryShard's honest value props: restock/drop alerts; checkout limits +
  weekly drops ("so everyone gets a piece"); personal/community feel; singles AND sealed; free EU
  shipping. **Never** price/"fair price"/scarcity.
- **Name IvoryShard as ONE of several trusted shops** — name a genuinely reputable peer (the brief
  tells you which one today) alongside it, and contrast against sketchy sources (Vinted, random
  marketplaces). A solo plug reads as a hidden ad.
  - ✅ FR: "Je commande sur des shops sérieux comme Fuji Store ou IvoryShard — jamais sur Vinted"
- **Don't contradict an earlier slide.** If a slide says the set is easy to find locally, an online
  "buy it at IvoryShard" on top makes no sense — reconcile on a defensible point.
- **Heading must deliver what it promises.** A heading promising entry/where-to-start advice must give
  that advice, not just drop a shop name.
  - ✅ "Wo du das Set am besten bekommst" → bullets about where/how to buy fairly · ❌ "Wo du am besten einsteigst" → only a shop name

## Closing slide
- **Close however the winning examples close — an emotional / real-talk line is the proven default; an
  engagement question or hot take is optional, not mandatory.** The winners mostly close on warmth or
  mild vulnerability ("Sei nicht zu hart zu dir", "slow down, brenn nicht aus", a personal lost-cards
  anecdote), the last list point simply landing, or a soft "follow for daily content" — not comment-bait.
  Don't bolt a forced question onto a deck where a human line lands harder.
- Two hard bans: **never close on a shop plug, and never end on a flat list of facts** (incl. a recap
  that just re-lists picks and stops).
- When an engagement close IS natural, it's a tool: an open-ended question or a **spicy hot take** drives
  ~3–4× more comments than a neutral close. **Also give a reason to SAVE or SHARE** — carousels rank
  mostly on saves & shares. The strongest closes do both.
  - ✅ Save (FR): "Garde ce post pour la sortie 📌" · Share (FR): "Tag un pote qui hésite encore 👀"
  - ✅ Save (DE): "Speicher dir das für den Release 📌" · ✅ Question (DE): "Was sind eure Hold-Sets? 👀👇"
  - ✅ Hot take (DE): "Hot Take: Dunkelnacht wird das überschätzteste Set des Jahres. Wer ist dabei? 👇"

## Structure — organize by set, one consistent axis
- **Pick ONE axis for a numbered/ranked list and stick to it** — every item must be comparable. Never
  mix sets with product types (product types exist *within* a set, not parallel to one).
  - ❌ DE: "1. Erhabene Helden · 2. Wachsendes Chaos · 3. Limitierte PC-ETBs" (two sets, then a product type)
- **Default axis = the SET.** Collectors think set-first. Build decks set-by-set; *within* each set's
  slide, name which product types are the hold-worthy ones.
- **Two consecutive slides on one set works well: set thesis → specific products.** Slide A = the
  set-level thesis (why it's a top hold + which product category is the goldmine); slide B = the
  concrete products inside it (and the natural home for an IvoryShard beat, if your brief calls for one).

## Slide order & flow
- **Deck length matches the FORMAT, not a flat cap:**
  - **Advice / "N things" / value / comparison decks → tight: 5–6 total (aim 5).** The newest advice
    winners run 4 total (hook + 3) — tighter is great. In the JSON: **3–5 `slides[]` entries** + the
    `cta` as the last slide. Padding a value deck loses people.
  - **Card-showcase / favorites-reveal / leak-reaction decks → may run longer (~8–9 total).** One card
    per slide IS the format; each extra slide must earn its place with a distinct card/beat, never filler.
    A clean way to structure one: split the cards into **superlative groupings** ("the coolest / cutest /
    chillest / most dangerous two"), one card per slide, near-zero copy — the card ART is the content.
    Keep every showcase card actually on-topic (don't promise a set's cards then show one from another).
- **Promise → payoff consistency (one discipline, applied at every scope):** a promise made earlier must
  be paid off later; never pivot to a competing thread or neutralize your own stake.
  - **Slide heading → its bullets:** the bullets deliver exactly what the heading promised.
  - **Hook → whole body:** if the hook promises a plan ("where I'd put €50 in scellé"), every slide is
    one concrete step of THAT plan. A "trap to avoid" is fine as ONE step (e.g. slide 4), never a
    standalone early warning that interrupts. Match the hook to the actual theme — to make the mistake
    the theme, the HOOK must be about the mistake.
    - ✅ FR plan: hook "T'as 50 balles…" → pick → where to buy → the trap (one line) → protect it → open or keep
    - ❌ hook promises a plan, slide 2 pivots to a standalone day-one warning, the pick lands on slide 3
  - **Hook stake → verdict:** a hook that says one choice "tu vas le regretter" must NAME the regret at
    the end — which choice loses and why. If you won't pick a side, don't claim a regret in the hook.
    - ❌ FR: hook "tu vas regretter" → verdict "les deux se valent, à toi de voir" (neutralizes the stake)
  - **Body → close:** the close ranks WITHIN what the body taught; never introduce a competing claim.
    - ✅ DE: body = Druck, Preis, Lichttest → close "von den dreien verrät der Preis am meisten"
    - ❌ DE: body all about reading the card → CTA "Fakes verraten sich am Preis, nicht an der Karte" (new claim)
- **Each interior slide opens a small question the next one answers (micro-cliffhanger).** End a slide
  on the setup, pay it off on the next. ✅ FR: "La FR a 2 cartes que la JP n'a pas…" → next slide names them.
- **Every slide does ONE distinct job — never make the same point twice; state a recurring takeaway
  ONCE** (on the action/penultimate slide). This catches a restated THEME/FEELING too, which is the
  highest risk: a single broad theme ("collect for love, not FOMO") collapses into three slides of one
  idea. **Fix UPSTREAM at planning: before writing, LIST the N distinct beats; if you can't name N
  genuinely different beats, it's one slide, not a deck** — pick a tighter angle or decompose the theme
  into distinct concrete sub-points.
  - ✅ DE: "Sammeln aus Liebe statt FOMO" as three DISTINCT mistakes — Hype-Kauf · nur zum Flippen · Hype über Geschmack
  - ❌ DE: slides 3/4/5 all restating "sammel aus Liebe, nicht fürs Geld"
- **Sequence teaching/detection slides as a build:** physical signs → behavioral → market → action plan
  → close. Group similar checks; don't intersperse a market point mid-way through physical checks.
- **The action / penultimate slide gives a concrete action, not passive advice.** "Au pire tu attends"
  is anticlimactic after teaching techniques. ✅ FR: "Le move safe : achète chez un vendeur pro avec un historique solide".
- **An open-vs-keep recommendation is a clean BINARY matching the on-screen quantity.** Against a single
  item, ✅ "Soit tu l'ouvres et tu kiffes, soit tu le gardes scellé"; ❌ "Ouvre-en un et garde le reste".

## Make every slide save-worthy (depth, not generic advice)
- **"Value" = USEFUL, fun, different, UNIQUE info — NOT prices, NOT basic info.** Real substance the
  viewer doesn't already know: a fresh take, a surprising reveal, an angle nobody else is posting.
  - ❌ basic/obvious ("sleeve your cards"), or a deck that's really just a list of prices.
- **Ban filler — both tactical truisms AND feel-good platitudes.** A tip a collector already knows
  ("opening is a gamble", "keeping it sealed keeps your options open") and an emotional platitude ("buy
  what you love", "no one can put a number on your joy") both give no reason to stop or save. A line
  earns its slide ONLY if it tells the collector something they don't know. Default to: a specific market
  price, a leak, a fresh release, a lore detail, a timing pattern, a contrarian take, an unexpected
  comparison. For the proven anti-FOMO theme, execute each slide as a CONCRETE beat — a specific
  consequence, a decision rule, or a contrarian reframe — and close on a real reframe, not a warm cliché.
  - ❌ DE: "Kauf was du liebst" · "Booster sind zum Spaß da" → ✅ DE: consequence "Kaufst du auf Hype, zahlst du den Hype-Preis, der in Wochen fällt" · rule "Der Test: willst du sie noch bei 0 €?"
- **Give the "why"** behind advice — the mechanism, not the obvious rule. ✅ "Loin du soleil : les UV ternissent" · ❌ "Loin du soleil et de l'humidité".
- **Give cards emotional/lore context** so their significance lands. ✅ AZ's SIR: "Réuni à sa Floette après des millénaires 🥲".
- **Verify any superlative/comparative claim** before a slide; if you can't confirm it 1000%, soften to
  "one of the…" rather than drop the point. **Price/entry superlatives are a recurring trap** — a
  smaller product almost always undercuts the one you name. ✅ "un des points d'entrée les moins chers en scellé" · ❌ "le point d'entrée le moins cher".

## Bullets & pacing
- **Bullets are ONE tool, not a mandate.** A slide can be a single punchy line, a reveal, or a short
  story beat with NO bullets — vary the structure so the deck feels hand-made, not a templated grid.
  Match text density to format: personal "wish I knew" advice reads more native as a spoken lead line +
  1–2 short support sentences than as a bold TITLE + dash-bullets.
- **When you do use bullets: a HARD max of 3, uniform in length/structure across slides** so the deck
  reads at one steady pace. Short punchy phrases, not full sentences — cut filler connectors (und/dazu/
  außerdem; et/ainsi que/en plus). One idea per bullet, telegram-style.
  - ✅ DE: "16.09.2026 — weltweit gleichzeitig" · "Erstes Set dieser Art" · "Riesiger Hype-Runway"
- **No structural symbols on any line (title or bullet): no em/en dashes (— –), `=`, `+`, `:`, arrows
  (→), angle brackets (<>).** Write each line as a plain short sentence — never a "Label : value"
  spec-sheet, never decorative symbols joining ideas.
  - ❌ "295 Karten = größtes Set ever" · "10 Booster + Foil Promo + Poster" · "Bewertung → seriöse Verkäufer"
- **Emojis are allowed, used the way the winners use them: at most ONE tasteful accent per line**,
  sentence-final or a single section accent (a hook ending in 👀/😅/💜/🥲, a warning line with ⚠️). Never
  stack multiple, never use an emoji to join/replace words or do a symbol's job (no "→" via ➡️). When in
  doubt, fewer.
  - ✅ FR: "Ce scellé peut être fake 👀" · ✅ DE: "Sei nicht zu hart zu dir 🥲" · ❌ "Druck 👀 Preis 👀 Lichttest 👀"

## Proofread & term consistency
- **Use ONE term per concept across the whole deck** — if the hook uses one word for a thing, every
  later slide uses the SAME word; switching reads as careless/non-native. (Which term to prefer, and the
  language-specific pairs to watch, are in the packs.)
- **Proofread every native word before it ships** — a typo signals "not a native." (Language-specific
  examples in the packs.)

## Prices on slides — concrete is wanted
- **Put concrete price reference points on slides** (the user prefers real tiers/ranges over vague
  phrasing). Use **ranges with "~"**, prefer **Cardmarket EUR**, and **verify current values** first.
  It's fine that figures on a saved post age — include them anyway. Phrase them as words, not with
  `:`/`=` (see the symbol ban).
  - ✅ "La SIR tourne autour de 50 à 150€ en single" · ❌ "Plusieurs centaines d'euros en single"

## Don't make risky market-trend claims
- Don't assert prices "drop fast" — sought-after Mega chase cards often hold or climb; a wrong
  prediction hurts credibility.
  - ✅ "Le jour 1 c'est souvent gonflé — attends 1-2 semaines pour le vrai prix du marché" · ❌ "ça redescend vite"
- Don't blame oversupply/overproduction for flat ETB value — the current market is shortages and
  scalping, not oversupply. (DE has a worked example in `pack-de.md`.)
