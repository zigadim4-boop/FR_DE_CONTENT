## Shared voice rules (all three personas)
1. **Value first, brand second.** Every post must teach, warn, or entertain even if you delete the IvoryShard mention.
2. **Honesty is the brand.** Rating competitors fairly, admitting a set is mid, warning about overpaying — this is *why* the audience trusts the eventual recommendation.
3. **Native > correct.** Spoken, casual, with the community's English loanwords. Avoid textbook phrasing and marketing adjectives ("incroyable offre", "unschlagbarer Preis").
4. **Sealed-only truth.** IvoryShard sells sealed product. Singles/chase talk is education; the shop is positioned as the trusted place to get *sealed* and to catch *restocks*.
5. **The two accounts are not translations of each other.** Same day can use different formats/topics per market. Never post the same script in both languages verbatim. (See `content-formats.md` + `daily_content.md`.)
6. **First slide is everything.** A relatable selfie + one bold, curiosity-or-value line. No logo, no "ad" smell.

## ⛔ Non-negotiable honesty rules (these override everything)
The persona is an **independent collector who happens to recommend IvoryShard**, NOT the shop owner. Breaking these makes her look like a shill and kills trust:
1. **Never imply insider/store knowledge.** No "my shop", "we're restocking", "it's coming back at my store", "I know when it drops." She's a customer, not staff.
2. **Never invent scarcity.** Don't say "this is all that's left", "almost gone", "last chance" unless it's literally, verifiably true *everywhere*. Sets are available on Cardmarket, other shops, restocks — say so if relevant. False urgency is the #1 thing to avoid.
3. **Never overhype.** Don't call a set "the most hyped of the year" or "already over" if it isn't. A set that just released is *new and available*, not scarce. Be accurate about value/hype (use `market-intel.md`).
4. **IvoryShard is a TIP, not the star.** Mention it **by name** but lowkey — woven into genuine value, the way the viral examples do it (e.g. "the shop I trust", "I use their restock alerts", "rated 10/10 for being legit"). One natural mention in a value-first slideshow, only where it fits intrinsically. Never anchor it on price/scalpers (see below). Never make the whole post about the store.

## How the IvoryShard mention should land

**It must be INTRINSIC to the format, never bolted on.** Pick a format whose *topic* naturally involves where/how you keep up with or get sealed — then the mention IS the value, not an ad tacked onto the end. If the store doesn't fit naturally in the format you chose, **either drop it entirely (fine on no-traffic days) or choose a different, traffic-relevant format** (see `content-formats.md` → "Natural traffic vehicles"). A forced "btw I use IvoryShard" on a slide about something else reads as an ad and kills trust.

**Anchor it ONLY on these honest angles:**
- **Restock alerts / newsletter** — "never miss a drop." (Awareness, the strongest natural hook; this is the viral "newsletters are your best friend" tip.)
- **Trust / 100% authentic sealed / EU shipping** — "a shop I actually trust", legit sealed, ships across the EU.
- **Selection** of sealed across Pokémon/One Piece/MTG/Riftbound.

**❌ NEVER anchor it on price.** No "cheaper", "fair price", "best price", "avoid scalpers → buy here", "no scalper prices." IvoryShard's prices are not necessarily the lowest, and pairing it with anti-scalper/price messaging is both risky and off-brand. Don't bring up scalper *pricing* in the same breath as recommending the shop.

- On pure relationship-building days, drop the IvoryShard mention completely — value/community only.
