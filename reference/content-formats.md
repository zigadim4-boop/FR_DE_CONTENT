# Content Format Library (Slideshows)

Net-new slideshow skeletons for Chloé (FR) and Lea (DE). Every format is built from the **DNA of the viral examples** but must stay **distinct from the 3 burned formats** below.

## ⛔ BURNED — study only, NEVER reuse (already cross-posted in both markets)
1. **"5 things I learned in 10 years of TCG"** listicle (selfie → numbered tips → IvoryShard as tip #3). *FR ss1/ss2 ↔ DE ss3.*
2. **"I rate the shops where I buy Pokémon cards"** (honest X/10 retailer ratings, IvoryShard 10/10 last). *FR ss3 ↔ DE ss2.*
3. **2-slide nostalgia** ("so glad I grew up with this…" / "they're so cool"). *DE ss1.*

Do not rebuild these with new wording. They're spent in both markets.

## ✅ The DNA every new format should keep
- **Slide 1 = relatable selfie + ONE bold line** that promises **value or curiosity** — a tip, a question, a relatable take. **Never** a store-status or scarcity hook ("this is all that's left", "it's almost back", "last chance"). The hook must work for someone who will never click the store. No logo.
- **Slide text density (match the examples):** every content slide has a **bold TITLE + 2–4 short bullet points** (and/or a reference photo with text around it) — NOT just a single line. Look at `examples/`: e.g. a numbered title then 3 punchy bullets. Sparse one-liners look unfinished.
- **At least 5 content slides** (6 incl. CTA is ideal). 3 tips is too few.
- Native voice + community slang (see `slang-fr.md` / `slang-de.md`).
- **Make tips DIVERSE — don't restate the same idea (e.g. price) five ways.** Across a list, vary the angle: what's the chase, which product gives the best odds, how to spot a fake, how to protect cards, how to buy fair, lore/collecting targets, etc.
- **Never say things that kill traffic or make no sense.** Avoid generic negatives like "opening it is a roulette" (every sealed product is — it's meaningless and discourages buying). **Reframe positively into useful value**, e.g. "which product gives the best statistical chance at the chase — ETB vs Booster Box vs Bundle?".
- **🔒 FACTUAL ACCURACY — every on-slide claim must be 1000% verified true (user, 2026-05-30).** The agent verifies BEFORE writing — never ship a "verify this before filming" note (that offloads it to the user). **No unofficial numbers on a slide:** Pokémon doesn't publish pull rates and community estimates disagree → **never put a numeric pull rate on a slide**; treat specific prices the same (volatile/stale). Use qualitative truths instead — "the rarest pull in the set", "the SIR is pulled far more often", "one of the priciest singles — check the current Cardmarket price". Slide-safe = official set names/dates, card names, rarity tiers, standard pack counts (Box 36 / ETB 9 / Bundle 6), that day's live stock, and plain logic ("more packs = more chances"). See memory `slide-facts-must-be-verified`.
- **Value/honesty first**, brand second. Soft CTA = follow + restock alerts/newsletter; stronger CTA only per `market-stock-logic.md`.
- Sealed-only: singles/chase talk is education; the buy/notify CTA points to sealed.
- Light Pokémon-starter mascot graphics + branded solid/pastel background optional.

## Natural traffic vehicles (where an IvoryShard mention is INTRINSIC, not bolted on)
Use these formats when the day needs traffic — the store's honest hooks (restock alerts, authenticity, trust) ARE the value:
- **"How I never miss a drop / restock"** — the whole tip is staying in the loop → "mes notifs sont sur IvoryShard." (This is the viral "newsletters are your best friend" idea as a full format.)
- **"How to make sure your sealed is legit / spot a fake sealed"** → ends on buying from a trusted, 100%-authentic shop.
- **"Where I keep up with new releases / how I shop sealed safely"** → trust + EU shipping + alerts.

If the format you picked is NOT one of these (e.g. a pull-odds comparison, a lore post), do **not** staple IvoryShard on — just leave it out for that post. A mention only earns its place when the slide's topic is about keeping up with / trusting / getting sealed. (Exception: even a non-store format can carry a *natural* stock heads-up when the products it discusses are literally in stock / coming soon — see the German pull-odds example.)

**Placement — exact rule (user, 2026-05-30):** put the natural IvoryShard touch mid-deck, not only on the final slide (viewers drop off before the end):
- **5-slide carousel → mention on slide 3.**
- **More than 5 slides → mention on slide 4 or later** (4th at the earliest, never before).
You can lightly reinforce it on the final/CTA slide. Still ONE genuine, natural touch — just positioned where people actually see it, and still subject to the "only if it fits naturally, else omit" rule.

## CTA snippets — pick by the product's LIVE availability (see market-stock-logic.md Step B2)
- **In stock now (FR):** "Si tu veux le tester, le [produit] est dispo là maintenant sur IvoryShard 👀" · "(DE) Falls du's willst: das [Produkt] ist gerade auf IvoryShard verfügbar 👀"
- **Coming soon (FR):** "Le [produit] arrive bientôt sur IvoryShard - active la notif pour pas le rater si t'en as pas encore." · "(DE) [Produkt] kommt bald wieder auf IvoryShard - Benachrichtigung an, dann bist du dabei."
- **Sold out / next drop (FR):** "Active les notifs de restock pour choper le prochain drop." · "(DE) Restock-Benachrichtigung an, dann verpasst du den nächsten Drop nicht."
- **Authenticity add-on (any):** "au moins le scellé est 100% authentique" · "(DE) das sealed ist 100% authentisch."

> ❌ Never: "almost gone / last chance" (false scarcity); never pair IvoryShard with price/"fair price"/"avoid scalpers"; never push a *restock alert* for something that's in stock right now. Match the ask to what the viewer can do today, and bridge it naturally from the content.

## Format catalogue (rotate; never the same format both markets same day)

### 1. "Pourquoi les nouveaux sets partent si vite" / "Warum neue Sets so schnell weg sind" — explainer (NOT fake scarcity)
**Use:** only when there's a real, observable rush (e.g. a live banner). Educational, not FOMO-baiting. Honest: the set is still available elsewhere.
- S1 (selfie): "Pourquoi les displays partent en quelques minutes 🤔" (curiosity, not "buy now")
- S2: the real reasons (print allocation, scalpers, hype)
- S3: how to NOT get scalped (check the cote yourself, be patient)
- S4: the smart play — wait for restocks, buy authentic sealed (generic; do NOT mention IvoryShard here — this is a price/scalper context where the store must not appear)
- S5: honest recap — "tu peux le trouver à plein d'endroits, vise juste le bon prix."

### 2. "Ne te fais pas avoir" / "Lass dich nicht abziehen" — anti-overpay price check
**Use:** when a product is being scalped above MSRP, or to build trust on a sold-out day. **Pure value — no IvoryShard mention** (price/scalper context).
- S1: "Paye JAMAIS ce [produit] plus de X€"
- S2: official price vs scalper price (screenshot Cardmarket trend)
- S3: how to check *la cote* yourself before buying
- S4: be patient — prices settle, restocks come, buy authentic
- S5: recap + generic "active tes notifs de restock" CTA (no store name).

### 3. "Ce que j'achèterais avec 50€" / "Was ich mit 50€ kaufen würde" — budget picks
**Use:** evergreen; great on sold-out days (pure value). Swap budget (30/50/100€).
- S1: "Si t'as 50€ à claquer en sealed, voilà quoi prendre"
- S2–S4: 2–3 sealed picks w/ a one-line "pourquoi" (hold potential, fun to open, chase odds)
- S5: which to keep sealed vs open + soft CTA.

### 4. "Hold or Brick?" — value verdict on current sets
**Use:** ties straight to `market-intel.md`. Authority builder.
- S1: "Ce set, tu HOLD ou c'est un brick? 🧐"
- S2–S4: 3 current products → HOLD / BRICK / WATCH with one reason each (print run, chase, demand)
- S5: "mon pick perso" + notif CTA.

### 5. "La chase de [set] vaut combien?" / "Was ist die Chase von [Set] wert?"
**Use:** new/hot set. Education on the headline card; funnels to sealed odds.
- S1: "Cette carte de [set] vaut déjà X€ 👀" (only if the price is verified that day; else go curiosity, not a number)
- S2: the chase card art + a verified price *range* or "check the live Cardmarket cote" (no invented figure)
- S3: how rare it is, QUALITATIVELY — "the rarest pull in the set / the SIR shows far more often." **Never a numeric pull rate** (unofficial, sources disagree).
- S4: cheapest fair way to chase it = sealed from a trusted shop
- S5: CTA per matrix.

### 6. "Mega-Évolution expliqué" / "Mega-Entwicklung erklärt" — era explainer
**Use:** onboarding newer collectors; evergreen during the Mega era.
- S1: "C'est quoi le délire des Méga-Évolutions en TCG?"
- S2–S4: what changed, which sets, which Mega cards are hyped
- S5: where to start (sealed) + follow CTA.

### 7. "Spot a fake en 10 sec" / "Fake in 10 Sek erkennen" — anti-counterfeit
**Use:** evergreen trust builder; strong saves/shares.
- S1: "Cette carte est fake et voilà comment je le sais"
- S2–S4: 3 tells (texture, light test, back color, font)
- S5: "achète scellé chez des vendeurs sérieux" + soft CTA.

### 8. "Compte à rebours 30 ans" / "30 Jahre Countdown" — anniversary hype
**Use:** building toward Oct 2026 worldwide Celebration set; recurring.
- S1: "Le plus gros set Pokémon de la décennie arrive…"
- S2–S4: what's coming, why it's huge (worldwide simultaneous, 30th)
- S5: "active les notifs pour le drop" CTA.

### 9. "FR/DE print vs JP" — comparison
**Use:** collector-nerd content; high engagement w/ serious collectors.
- S1: "Version FR ou JP, tu prends quoi?"
- S2–S4: print quality, price, availability, chase differences
- S5: verdict + (if relevant) what's in stock CTA.

### 10. "Restock storytime" — POV you almost missed it
**Use:** restock day / hype mode.
- S1: "POV: tu vois le mail de restock à 21h00 pile ⏰"
- S2–S4: the scramble, checkout limits = fair, you got one
- S5: "voilà comment être au courant en premier" → newsletter CTA (hard if stock supports).

### 11. "Top sets to hold right now" — ranking (not a shop rating!)
**Use:** authority; pure value. Distinct from the burned shop-rating format — this ranks **sets/products**, not retailers.
- S1: "Les sets que je garde scellés en ce moment"
- S2–S5: ranked sealed products w/ hold thesis; soft CTA at end.

### 12. "Sleeve/protect ton pull" — quick tutorial
**Use:** evergreen, beginner-friendly, saves/shares.
- S1: "T'as pull une alt? Fais ça TOUT DE SUITE"
- S2–S4: sleeve → toploader → away from light; cheap protection
- S5: soft community/follow CTA.
